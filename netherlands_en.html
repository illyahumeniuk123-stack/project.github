General Info

Why visit the Netherlands?

The Netherlands is a small but very special country in western Europe. It is famous for its pretty canals, bright fields of tulips, great museums, and open and friendly people. It is one of the best places to visit in Europe.

Cities like Amsterdam, Rotterdam, The Hague, and Delft are all very different and all very fun to visit. You can enjoy art, food, nature, bikes, and beautiful old buildings — all in one small country!

What makes the Netherlands
so special?

🌷
Tulip Fields

Keukenhof is the biggest flower garden in the world. Every spring, from March to May, millions of tulips make the land look like a big, colorful painting.

🏛️
Great Art Museums

The Rijksmuseum, the Van Gogh Museum, and the Mauritshuis have amazing paintings. You can see works by Rembrandt, Vermeer, and Van Gogh — all in one city!

🚲
Bike Culture

There are over 35,000 km of bike paths! Almost everyone in the Netherlands uses a bike every day. It is the best country in the world for cycling.

⚓
Canals and Old Houses

The canals of Amsterdam are a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The old, tall, narrow houses from the 1600s are very beautiful and easy to walk around.

🧀
Yummy Food

Try Gouda cheese, Edam cheese, fresh herring, stroopwafels (sweet syrup waffles), and Dutch fries with sauce. Simple but very tasty!

🌍
Open and Friendly People

The Netherlands is known for being a very free, safe, and welcoming country. People are kind and helpful. Almost everyone speaks good English!

Culture and Traditions

A Rich Culture

In the 1600s, the Netherlands was the richest country in the world. Dutch painters from this time are still very famous today. This time is called the "Golden Age."

🎨
Painting

Rembrandt, Vermeer, Hals — great Dutch painters from the past

🎭
Theatre

Over 60 theatres and many big events and festivals each year

🎺
Music

The Concertgebouw orchestra is one of the best in the world

👑
Holidays

King's Day on April 27 — the biggest national celebration

Key Dates

A Short History

Around 1200
Amsterdam is Founded

A small fishing village on the Amstel river slowly grew into the big and famous capital city we know today.

1568–1648
The Eighty Years' War

The Dutch people fought for their freedom from Spanish rule. In 1648, they won and became an independent country.

1600s
The Golden Age

The Netherlands became the richest country in the world. Trade, art, and science all grew very fast during this time.

1815
Kingdom of the Netherlands

After the wars with Napoleon, the Netherlands became a kingdom again. The king or queen still rules today.

1957
A Founder of the EU

The Netherlands was one of the six countries that started the European Economic Community — which later became the EU.

Useful Information

Tips for Travelers

  • 1
    Best Time to Visit April and May are great — the tulips are in bloom and the weather is nice. June to August is warm and there are many fun events.
  • 2
    Getting Around Rent a bike — it is the most fun and easiest way to see the city. Trains connect all the big cities quickly and cheaply.
  • 3
    Language People speak Dutch, but do not worry — about 95% of Dutch people speak very good English. You can talk to almost anyone!
  • 4
    Museum Card The Museumkaart gives you free entry to over 400 museums. It is a great deal if you love art and history.
  • 5
    Safety The Netherlands is one of the safest countries in Europe. Just be careful of bike lanes — bikes go fast and have the right of way!
✈️
How to Get There

Amsterdam Schiphol Airport is one of the biggest airports in Europe. You can fly there from many cities. You can also take a fast train from Brussels or Paris.

🏨
Where to Stay

Amsterdam — great for art and history lovers. The Hague — calm and elegant. Delft — small, cozy, and very pretty. Rotterdam — perfect for fans of modern buildings.
